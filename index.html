Personal Life

Subhas Chandra Bose was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, India, to Janakinath Bose and Prabhavati Devi. His father was a successful lawyer and his mother was a devout follower of the Hindu goddess Kali. Bose was the ninth child in a family of 14 children.

Bose completed his early education at an Anglo-Indian school in Cuttack and later went to Ravenshaw Collegiate School. In 1913, he moved to Calcutta (now Kolkata) to pursue higher studies. He studied at Presidency College and later at the University of Calcutta, where he obtained a degree in Philosophy.

In 1937, Bose married Emilie Schenkl, an Austrian woman whom he had met while studying in Europe. They had a daughter named Anita Bose Pfaff. Emilie and Anita lived in Austria, while Bose traveled extensively for his political work.

Bose was an active member of the Indian National Congress and served as the president of the party in 1938 and 1939. He was a strong supporter of complete independence from British rule and disagreed with the party's approach of seeking dominion status within the British Empire.

Bose was jailed by the British government several times for his political activities. In 1940, he was put under house arrest in Calcutta, but he managed to escape and flee to Germany, where he sought help from the Nazis to form an army to fight against the British. During his time in Germany and later in Japan, Bose continued to work towards Indian independence and formed the Indian National Army (INA), which fought alongside the Japanese army against the British in Burma and Northeast India.

In August 1945, Bose was reportedly killed in a plane crash in Taiwan, although there are conspiracy theories that suggest that he may have survived and lived in hiding. Bose remains a controversial figure in Indian history, with some hailing him as a hero and others criticizing his alliance with the Nazis and the Japanese. Despite the controversy surrounding his life and death, Bose's contribution to the Indian freedom movement is widely acknowledged. He remains a symbol of India's struggle for independence and is remembered for his fierce determination, courage, and patriotism.

Career

President of the Indian National Congress (1938 and 1939):

Bose was elected as the president of the Indian National Congress twice in 1938 and 1939. During his tenure, he advocated for complete independence from British rule and a more aggressive approach to achieving it.


Mayor of Calcutta (1930):

Bose was elected as the mayor of Calcutta in 1930. He implemented several measures aimed at improving the living conditions of the city's residents, including increasing the number of public parks, constructing new roads and buildings, and improving the city's water supply..


General Secretary of the Indian National Congress (1928-1929):

Bose was appointed as the general secretary of the Indian National Congress in 1928. He played a key role in organizing the annual sessions of the Congress and in formulating the party's policies and strategies.


Leader of the Forward Bloc (1939):

After resigning from the Indian National Congress in 1939, Bose formed a new political party called the Forward Bloc. He became its leader and aimed to create a united front against the British.


Member of the Bengal Legislative Council (1924-1928):

Bose was elected as a member of the Bengal Legislative Council in 1924. He used his position to advocate for the rights of Indian farmers and workers and to promote Indian culture and education.


Member of the Indian National Congress Working Committee (1929-1938):

Bose was a member of the Indian National Congress Working Committee from 1929 to 1938. He played a key role in formulating the party's policies and strategies, and advocated for a more aggressive approach to achieving India's independence.

Other works

Writing

Subhash Chandra Bose was a prolific writer and wrote several books throughout his life. Some of his notable books include:The Indian Struggle (1920-1934),My Ancestors,The Japanese and the Indian National Army,The Alternative Leadership.

Education

Subhash Chandra Bose was a highly educated person who completed his primary education at the Protestant European School in Cuttack, Odisha, and later studied at the Presidency College in Calcutta (now Kolkata), where he obtained a Bachelor of Arts degree in Philosophy in 1918. He also studied law at the University of Calcutta and went to England to prepare for the Indian Civil Service examination but later abandoned his studies to join India's freedom struggle.


Spirituality

Subhash Chandra Bose was a prominent Indian nationalist leader who had a strong interest in spirituality. He was raised in a family with a religious background and was influenced by the teachings of Swami Vivekananda and Sri Aurobindo. Bose explored various spiritual traditions, including Hinduism, and believed in the unity of all religions and the evolution of human consciousness..


Humanitarian work

Subhash Chandra Bose was a humanitarian who dedicated his life to serving the people of India. He established organizations to provide education, healthcare, and other basic necessities to marginalized communities, fought against social injustices, and established the Indian National Army (INA) to help India achieve independence and serve as a force for social justice and equality.
